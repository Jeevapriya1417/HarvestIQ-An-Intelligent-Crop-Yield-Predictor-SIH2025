# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

HarvestIQ is committed to providing a welcoming and inclusive environment for all community members. We value and respect contributions from people of all backgrounds and experiences, regardless of:

- Age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression
- Level of experience, education, socio-economic status
- Nationality, personal appearance, race, religion
- Sexual identity and orientation

We are dedicated to providing a harassment-free and inclusive experience for everyone.

---

## Expected Behavior

All community members are expected to:

### Be Respectful
- Treat all people with dignity and respect
- Listen actively and empathetically to others
- Value diverse perspectives and experiences

### Be Constructive
- Provide helpful and constructive feedback
- 🎯 Focus on ideas, not individuals
- Help create a positive learning environment

### Be Inclusive
- Welcome newcomers and help them get oriented
- Use inclusive language and avoid assumptions
- Make space for underrepresented voices

### Be Professional
- Keep discussions focused on the project
- Avoid political debates or off-topic discussions
- Maintain a professional tone in all interactions

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated:

❌ **Harassment and Intimidation**
- Offensive comments related to personal characteristics
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Threats or intimidating behavior
- Stalking or doxxing

❌ **Discrimination**
- Exclusion based on identity or background
- Discriminatory language or jokes
- Unwelcome comments about appearance or identity

❌ **Disruptive Conduct**
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Spam or excessive self-promotion
- Deliberate disruption of discussions
- Publishing private information without consent

❌ **Violation of Privacy**
- Sharing others' personal information
- Recording conversations without consent
- Photographing without permission

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All project spaces (GitHub, discussions, issues, pull requests)
- Community events and conferences
- Public spaces where individuals represent the project
- Online and offline interactions

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. **Document** the incident (date, time, people involved, description)
2. **Report** to the maintainers:
   - Email: conduct@harvestiq.com
   - Private message on GitHub
3. **Follow up** - maintainers will respond within 48 hours

**Note**: All reports are confidential. When you report an issue, please include:
- Your contact information
- Names of people involved
- Your account/username
- Description of what happened
- Any additional context or witnesses

### Investigation & Response

The maintainers will:

1. Acknowledge receipt within 48 hours
2. Investigate the report thoroughly
3. Determine if a violation occurred
4. Take appropriate action
5. Inform the reporter of the outcome (timeline permitting)

### Consequences

Violations may result in:

1. **Warning** - First time, minor violation
2. **Temporary Ban** - 7-30 days from project spaces
3. **Permanent Ban** - Repeated or severe violations
4. **Legal Action** - If warranted and appropriate

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate corrective action
- Treating all reports fairly and confidentially
- Modeling positive community behavior
- Providing resources and support

## Scope of Violations

### Inside Project Scope
- GitHub issues, pull requests, and discussions
- Project documentation and wiki
- Code reviews and comments
- Private messages about project matters

### Outside Project Scope (May Affect Membership)
- Severe harassment of project members
- Discrimination based on involvement in the project
- Actions that create a hostile environment with intent

## Examples of Positive Behavior

✅ **Comments that Strengthen Our Community**

```
"Great question! Here's how I would approach this..."

"I see your point. Let me offer another perspective..."

"This is a thoughtful approach. We could also consider..."

"Thank you for the contribution! A few suggestions..."

"I'm new to this project. Could someone help me get started?"

"The community helped me so much. I want to give back!"
```

---

## Examples of Unacceptable Behavior

❌ **Comments that Violate Our Code**

```
"This is a stupid question." (Insulting)

"Women aren't good at coding like men." (Discriminatory)

"If you can't figure this out, you shouldn't be here." (Hostile)

"I published your email address on Twitter." (Privacy violation)

"You're too X to contribute here." (Harassment)
```

---

## Conflict Resolution

When disagreements arise:

1. **Assume Good Intent** - Assume others are trying their best
2. **Communicate Clearly** - Express concerns directly and respectfully
3. **Listen Actively** - Understand the other person's perspective
4. **Find Common Ground** - Look for shared values and goals
5. **Be Willing to Apologize** - Admit mistakes and learn from them
6. **Escalate if Needed** - Involve maintainers if you can't resolve it
